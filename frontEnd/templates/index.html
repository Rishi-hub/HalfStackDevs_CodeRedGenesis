Conoco Philips Sustainability

Welcome to ConocoPhillips' sustainability tracking page. We are devoted to monitoring and improving the environmental impact of our global operations.

Sustainability Scores

Total Sustainability Scores (Out of 100)

  1. Asia Pacific89
  2. Europe / Middle East61
  3. U.S.A.32
  4. Canada25

Sustainability scores are produced by a weighted average of 5 metrics: Energy Utiization, Oil Spills, Water Usage, Waste Production, and Greenhouse Emissions. Each metric is calculated as having a score between 0-20 for any particular metric. Here, lower values indicate unsustainability, and higher values indicate sustainability. From this, the reciprocal of this value is taken, and we are given a decimal value as a score for the metric as a perentage. Utilizing a scientific weighing of sustainability measures we produce a weighted average for the final sustainability score.

Energy Expenditure

Energy Sustainability Scores (Out of 20)

  1. Europe / Middle East15
  2. Asia Pacific14
  3. U.S.A.13
  4. Canada0

We use Energy Expenditure in Trillions of BTU as a core metric for determining sustainability, especially in the context of energy-intensive industries. This metric is particularly telling in the oil and gas sector, where energy consumption is directly tied to production levels. For oil and gas companies, this is not just a measure of efficiency, but a reflection of their commitment to sustainable practices.

Oil Pollution

Spillage Sustainability Scores (Out of 20)

  1. Asia Pacific20
  2. Europe / Middle East20
  3. U.S.A.2
  4. Canada0

We measure this impact using a simple yet effective metric: the number of barrels spilled minus the number of barrels recovered, termed as 'net spillage'. This figure directly correlates to environmental health; higher net spillage indicates a greater negative impact on ecosystems and sustainability. It highlights the urgency for effective spill management and recovery efforts in the oil and gas industry. By striving to minimize net spillage, we commit to protecting our planet and ensuring sustainable practices in our operations.

Water Consumption

Water Sustainability Scores (Out of 20)

  1. Asia Pacific20
  2. Canada15
  3. U.S.A.7
  4. Europe / Middle East0

Our Water Sustainability Score is calculated by adding total tons of water withdrawn to one million times the hydrocarbons in overboard discharges. This formula captures the extensive impact of water contamination - research indicates that one ton of contaminants can adversely affect at least one million tons of surface water. Therefore, a higher score reflects a more significant negative impact on environmental sustainability. This score serves as a crucial indicator, guiding us towards more responsible water use and better protection of our aquatic ecosystems.

Waste Production

Waste Sustainability Scores (Out of 20)

  1. Asia Pacific20
  2. Europe / Middle East11
  3. Canada10
  4. U.S.A.0

Our metric for assessing this is 'Net Waste', calculated by subtracting the amount of waste disposed from the total waste produced. This figure is a key indicator of environmental stewardship; a higher net waste value signifies a larger ecological footprint and a move away from sustainability. It underscores the urgency of enhancing waste reduction and disposal strategies. By diligently working to lower our net waste, we are taking concrete steps towards more sustainable and responsible resource management.

The focus on reducing these emissions is essential in our pursuit of sustainable development.
